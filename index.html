Bio

Adam Bomb (Adam Brenner) began his career as a teenage guitar prodigy in Seattle, quickly gaining attention for his explosive playing style and flamboyant stage presence. By the age of 16 he was performing with members of TKO and Queensrÿche, before moving to New York City to fully immerse himself in the rock scene.

Throughout the 1980s he became a cult figure in the glam and hard‑rock underground, collaborating with members of KISS, Aerosmith, Black ’n Blue, and even John Paul Jones of Led Zeppelin. His debut album Fatal Attraction became a fan‑favorite, followed by a long string of releases that showcased his mix of glam, punk attitude, and high‑energy guitar theatrics.

Over the decades Adam Bomb toured relentlessly across Europe, the United States, and UK, often armed with his signature pyrotechnic guitar show. His discography spans glam metal, raw rock ’n’ roll, acoustic sessions, and live albums that capture the chaos and electricity of his performances.

In 2026 he returns to Europe with the Bang Up Summer Tour, including special guest appearances supporting Scorpions in France. Still loud, still wild, still on fire — Adam Bomb remains one of rock’s most unstoppable forces.
